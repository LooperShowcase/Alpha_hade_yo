Koenigsegg

Koenigsegg Automotive AB is a Swedish manufacturer of high-performance sports cars based in Ängelholm, Skåne County, Sweden.

Koenigsegg cars have broken various production car speed records. The company was founded in 1994 by Christian von Koenigsegg. Many years of testing led to the CC8S, their first street-legal production car.

The CCX, introduced in 2006, used an in-house engine to meet global regulations. The CCXR was named one of "the world's most beautiful cars" by Forbes in 2009.

Koenigsegg focuses on innovation — from flex-fuel "Flower Power" engines to camless technology found in the Gemera (2020). Most parts are made in-house. In 2019, a 20% stake was sold to NEVS for $171 million.
